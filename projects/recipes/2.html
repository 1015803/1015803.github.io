Shepherd's Pie

Shepherds pie is a Scottish recipe that is often made in my family. Although my family doesn't have a specific family recipe unlike for shortbread it is still a staple of Scottish heritage in my family.

Recipe
Time
Cook Time: 40 mins
Prep Time: 15 mins
Total Time: 1 hour and 5 mins

For this recipe you will need:

  • 1½ pounds (700 g) potatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 4 tablespoons butter, divided
  • 1/4 cup milk (adjust for desired mash consistency)
  • 1 lb (450 g) ground lamb (or ground beef for a cottage pie)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 cup beef or lamb stock
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme (or 1 tablespoon fresh)
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • Olive oil or vegetable oil for cooking
  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Boil the potatoes in salted water until tender, about 15–20 minutes. Drain and mash with 2 tablespoons of butter and the milk; season with salt and pepper. Set aside.
  3. While the potatoes cook, heat 1–2 tablespoons oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the onion and carrots and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add the ground lamb and cook, breaking it up with a spoon, until browned and cooked through. Drain any excess fat if necessary.
  5. Stir in the tomato paste, stock, Worcestershire sauce, thyme, salt, and pepper. Simmer until the mixture thickens slightly, 5–8 minutes. Stir in the peas and remove from heat.
  6. Transfer the meat mixture to a baking dish (about 9x13-inch or similar). Spread the mashed potatoes evenly over the top; use a fork to texture the surface and dot with the remaining butter.
  7. Bake for 20–25 minutes, until the top is golden and the filling is bubbling. For a more browned top, place under the broiler for 2–3 minutes—watch closely to avoid burning.
  8. Let the shepherd's pie rest for 5–10 minutes before serving. Enjoy!
